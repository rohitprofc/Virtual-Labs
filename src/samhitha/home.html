Description for calculating the Area of a Triangle using Heron's formula:

Heron's formula is used to find the area of a triangle that has three sides. It is written as, Area = sqrt[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)], where a, b, and c are the sides of the triangle, and 's' is the semi-perimeter of the triangle i.e., s = a + b + c.

Here, first you want to enter the length of the sides of the triangle to calculate the area, and there are two special cases:
Case 1: The sum of the lengths of any two sides is equal to the length of the third side. In this case, the area is "zero"
Case 2: The sum of the lengths of any two sides is less than the length of the third side. In this case, the area is "NaN"
